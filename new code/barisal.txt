Barisal District, officially spelled Barishal District from April 2018, is a district in south-central Bangladesh, formerly known as Bakerganj district. Here's a summary of key information about Barisal District and its subdivisions:

1. **Location**: Barisal District is located in the Barisal Division of Bangladesh. The district's headquarters is the city of Barisal, which also serves as the headquarters of Barisal Division.

2. **History**: Barisal District traces its origins to Bakerganj district, which was established in 1797. It was placed in Barisal Division on January 1, 1993.

3. **Education**: The district is home to several notable educational institutions, including Sher-e-Bangla Medical College, University of Barisal, Barisal Engineering College, Barisal Cadet College, and others.

4. **Demographics**: As of the 2022 census, Barisal District had a population of 2,570,450, with a population density of 923 people per square kilometer. The literacy rate (age 7 and over) in the district was 79.6%.

5. **Religion**: Islam is the predominant religion in the district, with a significant Hindu population. In 2011, 11.69% of the population was Hindu, 0.53% was Christian, and a small percentage followed other or unspecified religions.

6. **Subdivisions**: Barisal District is divided into several Upazilas (formerly called Thanas), including Agailjhara Upazila, Babuganj Upazila, Bakerganj Upazila, Banaripara Upazila, Barisal Sadar Upazila, Gournadi Upazila, Hizla Upazila, Mehendiganj Upazila, Muladi Upazila, and Wazirpur Upazila.

7. **Notable Personalities**: Barisal District has been home to several notable figures, including politicians, musicians, philosophers, social workers, and more.
